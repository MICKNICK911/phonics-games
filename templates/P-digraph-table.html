How to Use This Resource

Welcome to the Digraphs educational tool! This resource helps you learn and practice consonant digraphs, which are two or more letters that together represent a single sound.

Understanding Digraphs

  • Digraphs are combinations of two letters that make one sound.
  • Some common digraphs include: sh, ch, th, wh, ph, ck
  • Some digraphs like "th" can make different sounds (as in "that" vs "thin")

How to Practice

  • Read the words in the tables aloud, focusing on the digraph sounds.
  • Use the interactive game section to test your knowledge.
  • Click on letters to highlight them, then click "Check Word" to see if you've found a digraph.
  • You have 3 attempts per word to find all digraphs.

Tips for Success

  • Practice a little every day for best results.
  • Say the words out loud to reinforce the sounds.
  • Try to think of other words that use the same digraphs.
